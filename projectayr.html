About Ayr

Project Ayr came to mind after countless hours playing Aurora 4x and Children of a Dead Earth. I had already written a ballistics simulation that could be used for armor and high velocity projectile calculations. I just needed to add a game on top of it.

After a couple of months, I had an ugly looking prototype that has one of the most complex ecological simulations in a game. But I realize I can do far better, and the design was not scalable to large universe sizes.

In comes this project, Project Ayr. Being written in Unity using the Entity Component System and utilizing compute shaders for ecological simulation work, the scalability of the simulation will be hundreds of times better.

The idea is a long-play roguelike 4x game where you design ships, manage colonies, conduct diplomacy, and fight to control the part of the galaxy you are in. The universe is generated as you visit new systems and around every corner there may be a superior bloodthirsty civilization waiting to hit you with kinetic impacts until the surface of you world is molten. Or there might be even worse lurking just beyong your vision.

The game will provide many ways to set policies and automate your civilization. The AI required isn't overly complex, and this is probably the simplest project I am working on.
